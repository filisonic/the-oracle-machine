Introduction

The Oracle Machine is a kinetic installation that responds to presence and sound. It cycles through five behavioural states—Dormant, Attentive, Overwhelmed, Receptive, Withdrawn—each governing how it listens, moves, and whether it will accept a consultation. The work explores thresholds between attention and withdrawal, and the ritual of seeking an answer from a system that may refuse to answer. This dossier provides technical and spatial requirements for curators and venues.

Behavioural states
  • I. DormantThe machine rests. Fans closed. No query will be received.
  • II. AttentiveSomething has been sensed. The system orients without committing.
  • III. OverwhelmedPresence is too insistent. The oracle withdraws into itself.
  • IV. ReceptiveA threshold has been crossed. The consultation may begin.
  • V. WithdrawnThe oracle has closed. Come back later, differently.

Space & environment

Minimum clear floor area: 120 × 120 cm; clear height 2.2 m (7 ft) recommended. The work occupies approximately 120 × 120 cm footprint and stands approximately 7 ft high. Dim or controllable lighting is recommended to support the installation’s presence.

Installation & operation

Setup: Installation and teardown by artist or designated technician. Venue to provide power (24 V 1200 W supply or adequate outlets as specified), clear floor area, and recommended clear height.

Provided by artist: The Oracle Machine (kinetic structure, electronics, control unit), power supply, C-stands.

Provided by venue: Power, minimum space as above, optional plinth or floor as agreed.

Operation: No daily maintenance required; contact artist or technician for any technical issues.
